TAI Fellowship 2026

We are selecting 6 inaugural fellows for a fully funded, 6-month program building the next generation of leaders at the intersection of technology, policy, and human rights.

The opportunity

What the Fellowship offers

The TAI Fellowship is designed to accelerate the careers of exceptional students who want to shape how technology is governed. Over six months, you will publish meaningful research, build a professional profile, and gain access to networks and mentors that would otherwise take years to reach.

Fellows are mentored directly by TAI's advisory board and extended network — learning everything we know, and working alongside experienced practitioners in AI governance, digital rights, law, and policy. You will publish articles at the intersection of technology and your area of interest, either independently or co-authored with your mentors and research collaborators. Your work will be translated internationally through the Burlington Group.

◈

Fully funded

Competitive stipend in USD, adjusted based on years of experience and expertise level. All conference travel and networking costs covered.

◈

Publish your work

Write and publish articles in your area of interest within the TAI context. Publish solo or jointly with mentors and research collaborators.

◈

Mentorship & network

Direct mentorship from advisory board members and access to a growing network of practitioners across AI policy, law, and governance.

◈

Conferences & exposure

Attend conferences, roundtables, and convenings. Build the relationships and visibility that open doors in the tech policy space.

◈

Laptop provided

Each fellow receives a laptop to support their research and work throughout the fellowship period.

◈

Certificate of completion

All finalists receive a certificate recognizing their participation and contributions to the inaugural TAI Fellowship cohort.

Who should apply

Eligibility

We are looking for 6 exceptional students — across undergraduate, masters, and doctoral levels — who are working on something connected to the TAI mission.

Currently enrolled as a student — undergraduate, masters, or PhD
Working in or studying technology, law, finance, policy, or economics
Demonstrated commitment to human rights and upholding the rule of law
Interested in publishing research at the intersection of technology and governance

How it works

Selection process

The fellowship selection is rigorous and designed to identify candidates who are not only talented but genuinely committed to the work.

1

Application

Submit your CV and one paragraph explaining what makes you exceptional.

2

Written essay

Shortlisted candidates will be invited to submit a written essay on a topic within the TAI mandate.

3

Interview rounds

Three stages of interviews to assess your thinking, motivation, and fit with the fellowship's mission.

4

Paid task

Finalists complete a compensated assignment — a real piece of work that demonstrates your capabilities.
